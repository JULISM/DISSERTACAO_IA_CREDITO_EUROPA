# Artificial Intelligence and Non-Performing Loans in the European Banking System

This repository contains the data, code, and supporting materials for my Master's dissertation, which investigates the relationship between Artificial Intelligence (AI) investment and Non-Performing Loans (NPLs) in the European banking system.

The study examines twelve countries participating in the Single Supervisory Mechanism (SSM) between 2015 and 2023, using panel data econometric techniques, including Fixed Effects and System Generalized Method of Moments (System GMM) models. AI investment is measured through venture capital funding directed toward AI companies, while banking sector credit quality is assessed through the NPL ratio obtained from the European Central Bank.

The research explores whether greater investment in AI-driven ecosystems is associated with lower levels of bank default, contributing to the literature on financial intermediation, credit risk assessment, machine learning, and banking stability.

**Keywords:** Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Non-Performing Loans (NPL), Credit Risk Assessment, Banking Stability, Financial Intermediation, European Banking System, Panel Data Analysis, System GMM.
